Love Addict? No Thank You

By Sara Thursday, April 30, 2015 Life After Married, RELATIONSHIPS, Uncategorized Permalink 1

Occasionally I get a text or email from him. I’d say it’s when I least expect it, but I never really do expect them, so their arrival shocks my system, like an unexpected bolt of lightening on a dull, cloudy day.  Seeing the once familiar name, so awkward and foreign to me now, sets off a chain reaction in my nervous system.  …wait, where am i…what day is it what month is it…is this real or am i dreaming…no, i’m not…this is where i am…this is who i am… The adrenaline surges, I get flashes of silver snakes in my peripheral vision, and my heart starts beating rapidly.  My fight or flight mechanism is in full operational mode… why are you doing this…what do you want…see, I knew you couldn’t forget me…i hate you… leave me alone…i miss you what are you doing…are you sorry you let me go…i’m so fine without you…i never want to see you again…will you answer if i call…why are you texting me…should i text you back…and it takes me a few minutes to breathe myself to a place of calm and equanimity.

Love Addict? No Thank You

The texts are simple and direct, lacking the nuance and innuendo that I choose to read into them, but the intimation of a deeper meaning, of a cryptic message embedded within the generic salutations, protects my reconditioned heart like an invisible warrior’s shield.  …what do you want…why are you texting me…i have nothing to give you…you broke me once i won’t let you in again…go away and let me be… I hate that I allow myself to react, hate that the neurons in my brain trigger a surge of energy that makes my palms moist and my ears hot. I hate knowing that my day will be filled with thoughts of him, and that I allowed the thief of thoughts to steal my precious calm.

I take my memories and spill them on the table, slowly taking stock of what is there, scraping the loose fragments and stray images into a giant pile, trying to make sense of what is there.  I run my straight edge around each side, pushing, piling, scraping, sorting, chopping, straightening, equalizing, until everything is neat and tidy and contained.  And there it sits, this pile of addiction, glaring and obvious, taunting me to take a taste.  …just one taste…just one whiff…it’s harmless and you’ll feel so alive…so energized…remember how engorged your senses were, how divine and awake you felt…that heady, burgeoning sensation…you can fly…go places you didn’t know existed…see the brightest lights, the richest hues… and then I breathe deeply and remember the feeling once the sensationalized moment has passed.

The hangover. The emptiness.  The deafening quiet waiting for the phone to chirp or the computer to ping. The agony of being trapped alone in your head.  The solitude of cleaning up the mess of broken promises and broken lives. The pounding headache waiting for the nerves to deaden and the veins to stop pumping excess emotion. The ownership of actions that required two but only one showed up to be held accountable. The exhaustion and bitterness once the wave of indulgence has returned to the sea, leaving claw marks in the sand as a reminder of what happened.

Pride is a more powerful aphrodisiac than love.  Respect lasts longer than the heady sensation of feeling ignited. Peace and security – stability – hold more meaning than the thrill of the moment.  I can read these messages and stand apart from them, knowing how valuable trust and equanimity truly are.  …there is nothing here…nothing to say…no place to go…no satisfaction or real happiness…don’t step backwards or off the cliff…peace was so hard won…don’t be a fool… I take a deep breath to the bottom of my lungs, exhaling to a place of strength while my finger grazes the delete key.
