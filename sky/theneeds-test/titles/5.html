These two wrist wearables feature watches with a twist

by Stanley Goodner on May 29, 2015 at 3:46 pm

Sections: Accessories, Communications, Mobile, Wearable Tech
    0

      With the amount of attention and hype backing smartwatches from major companies throughout this past year, it’s pretty easy to feel fatigued from it all. the marketing pressure from smartphones, tablets, PCs/laptops, and everything else can be bad enough. On top of all that, smartwatches add another tier for spec-wars, generational upgrading, and all the accessories that ‘must’ go with it.

      Though it seems like so much is going on, if there was ever a time to pay more attention instead of less, it’s now! Not everyone wants a smartwatch, sure. But a good wrist-mounted gadget? That’s a different story. And there’s a lot of innovation going on when you look past what Apple, Samsung, and others are creating.

      The Cogito Fit, which is currently funding on Kickstarter, identifies itself as a “watch with some smarts”. Give it only a quick glance, and you might mistake it for a good-looking yet standard watch. There is no LED/LCD screen, no touch functionality, and no port to plug/charge. And the Cogito Fit does run off a CR2032 battery, and it is water resistant.

      So how is it smart? The Cogito Fit pairs with your mobile device and notifies via blinking and vibrations you when you receive: emails, text messages, phone calls, and more. It also features camera shutter, music track control, and some activity monitoring with the free app for iOS and Android. This is the kind of smart watch for those who only want to see the time and/or know when to pull out their smartphone.

      But maybe you don’t need notifications, since your smartphone is always in-hand. So how about some extra local storage worn on the wrist, instead? That’s the core feature behind the ReVault smartwatch, which has blasted past its funding goal on Indiegogo. ReVault ups the tech factor with 32GB or 128GB of space, a digital screen face, and Bluetooth and WiFi connectivity.

      The ReVault app, available for iOS, Android, Windows, Mac OS X, and Linux, lets authorized users save and access data on the smartwatch. Internet access is not required, making the ReVault your personal, secure cloud storage. Automatically backup and sync files across multiple devices without any costs involved.

      ReVault comes with a choice of a watch strap, but any standard watch band will work. Not a fan of stuff on your wrist? You can choose to carry ReVault as a pocketwatch, on a keyring, or even on a chain around your neck. It’s like having all the benefit of OTG USB storage without any of the hassle of a physical/cable connection.

      So go and check out the Cogito Fit and ReVault smartwatch before their campaigns come to a close!
